The True Cost of Freelancing: Expenses You're Probably Forgetting

June 16, 2026 · 7 min read

When I first started freelancing, I thought my expenses were simple. A laptop. An internet bill. Maybe some software.

I was wrong. And I undercharged for two years because of it.

Here's the thing about freelance expenses: they're not just the obvious stuff. They're the $15/month tools that quietly add up to $600 a year. The hardware you replace every 3 years. The professional liability insurance you hope you never need. The coffee shops where you hold client meetings.

This article is a complete inventory of what freelancers actually spend — organized by category with real dollar amounts. Use it as a checklist the next time you set your rate.

This is where expenses sneak up on you. A tool here, a subscription there. Individually they're nothing. Together they're a mortgage payment.

"A $15/month tool isn't $15. It's $180 a year. Before you subscribe, ask yourself: will this generate $180 more in revenue? If not, skip it."

6. Expenses by Profession

Your profession determines which of these categories apply. A virtual assistant spends almost nothing on software ($45/month). An SEO specialist burns through $380/month on keyword tools and crawlers.

That's why our calculator uses different expense defaults for each profession — and why we're adding more every two weeks. A photographer's overhead is completely different from a copywriter's.

Here's a quick comparison of monthly expenses across some of the professions in our directory:

The takeaway? Don't copy someone else's rate. Their expenses are different from yours. Run your own numbers.
